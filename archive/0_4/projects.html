Past Projects


Along with the redesign of this website over the years, I have also worked on the following projects:

Menomonee Falls Teen Center

Details: The Menomonee Falls Teen Center had about 10-16 computers that were not networked together. These computers had problems with teens swearing using the AIM messager, and offensive backgrounds were used on some of the computers. Also, there was software on some computers and not others. Spyware clogged many of the systems, making them not workable for some time.

The Fix: I networked all computers together using a web structure, I had one computer that had a printer and gave all computers access to share the printer. I also shared application folders so that the computers could all share software. I created a Reg file that prohibited changes to the desktop background. I developed a background that was clean looking and advertised the teen center. I created a screen saver that also was pleasing to the eye, and promoted the center. I also built an application that detected key strokes of the user. I would store the typed words in ram, and found swear words. If someone would swear, a full screen pop up would appear for 30 seconds reminding the person that swearing was not allowed. It also gave time for the staff to see if people were swearing. I also installed Spybot search and destroy on the computers to remove the spyware and used Spyware blaster to prevent spyware to be installed. (As a side note, after the system was complete , the school district then donated locked down error pron computers that were not compatible with my software and network.)

Personal: Math Problems

Details: One of my close family members had problems with there multiplication tables. They need a entertaining way to memorize there math tables

The Fix: I developed 2 applications for my family member. Tic Tac Multiply and Tic Tac Divide. It used MS Agent technology to fly around the screen and play Tic Tac Toe using math problems. The text to speech personalized every game, and the built in win lose progress report gave me info as to how they were doing.

Personal: family members computer

Details: my cousin's computer crashed due to 13 different viruses.

The Fix: There were many programs that they had, that were only factory installed. I attempted to fix the problem firstly by running antivirus, but the virsuses corrupted the files so much, that after the virsus scan, the computer would not boot. The Master Boot sector was corrupt, along with Windows XP start up program and the Reg. I ran a copy of Knoppix (Linux bootable CD) to back up any personal files that they may have needed. I then Reformatted the Hard Drive and installed a copy of Windows on the machine along with needed updates and accesories such as Real One player and Direct X . I did not have copys of MS Office, or any of the other programs they originally had, so I installed the following as replacements:

- OpenOffice.org to replace MS Office
- AntiVir to replace McAfee Antivirus
- Gaim to replace MSN/AOL/Yahoo Messagers
- Gimp as a paintshop alternative
- Celestria as a MS Astrology Replacement
- Iceows to replace WinZip
- Frozen bubble for fun

To my knowledge, the system has not had a problem since the fix.
